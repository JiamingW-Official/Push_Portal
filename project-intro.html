PROJECT INTRODUCTION

Push is infrastructure, not a marketplace.

Most platforms help merchants and creators find each other. Push helps them transact reliably, measure results, and repeat. That's a different business — and a more defensible one.

SCROLL
ROOT CAUSE

The same broken system hurts both sides.

🏪 MERCHANT PROBLEMS
Follower count is a useless signal
Merchants evaluate creators by followers, aesthetics, and "vibes." None of these predict whether a creator will show up, post on time, or actually drive real customers.
Root cause: there's no standardized creator credit system for local commerce.
ROI is invisible
After spending $150 on a creator, merchants can't answer: did anyone actually come because of that post? Was the campaign worth it? Who should I work with again?
Root cause: no attribution layer exists in informal creator deals.
Every collaboration is manual
Outreach, negotiation, content approval, scheduling, reminders, proof tracking, payments — all managed through DMs and spreadsheets. For a cafe owner running their own business, this is unsustainable.
Root cause: creator collaboration is treated as ad hoc relationship management.
No protection when things go wrong
Creators take free product and disappear. Content goes up and then gets deleted. No-shows happen with no consequence. There's no escrow, no dispute path, no accountability.
Root cause: no consequence-backed trust system.
🎥 CREATOR PROBLEMS
No pricing power
Rising creators cannot prove their commercial worth. Every merchant offers whatever they feel like, and without a verifiable track record, creators negotiate from zero every single time. The market has no memory.
Root cause: no portable, verifiable creator performance history exists.
Trapped in freebie loops
Most gigs offer free product in exchange for a post. There's no structured path from doing a free shoot for a café to getting paid $50 a campaign to being on retainer with premium brands. Platforms don't distinguish onboarding from growth.
Root cause: no career progression framework in creator-merchant commerce.
Income is unpredictable
Random DMs, inconsistent payments, vague requirements that change after the work is done. Creators have no pipeline, no recurring relationships, no predictable income. Every month starts from scratch.
Root cause: the market has no pipeline infrastructure for creators.
No accountability on the merchant side
Merchants can reject work arbitrarily, change requirements mid-campaign, or simply never respond. Creators have no recourse. The power imbalance is structural.
Root cause: no bilateral accountability layer exists in informal creator deals.

Push Score = (Completion × 0.30) + (Reliability × 0.20) + (Quality × 0.25) + (Satisfaction × 0.15) + (Engagement × 0.10)

STRATEGIC ARCHITECTURE

Five systems. One infrastructure.

01
Offer OS
For merchants — goal to structured campaign
Merchants choose a business objective (drive weekday traffic, launch a new item, fill an event, increase first-time visits). The system converts it into a complete campaign framework: offer, payout, creator requirements, verification method, standby settings. No more blank-slate campaign creation.
02
Creator Credit
For creators and merchants — performance history as currency
Creators build a performance track record through every campaign: completion rate, reliability, quality ratings, merchant satisfaction, engagement. Higher scores unlock better campaigns, higher payouts, and priority matching. Scores decay with inactivity — maintaining standing requires consistent performance.
03
Attribution
For everyone — did the campaign actually work?
5 verification layers: (1) fulfillment evidence (timestamps, merchant acknowledgement), (2) content verification (live URLs, persistence checks), (3) engagement context (views, saves — directional only), (4) merchant-side action (redemptions, bookings, offer claims), (5) repeat signal (merchant rebooking, creator invited to premium campaigns). Each layer adds confidence. Confidence determines what you can claim.
04
Demand Surface
The live map — real-time opportunities
Creators see exactly what's available: distance, payout, slots remaining, deadline, tier required, offer details. Not a discovery map — a commercial interface. Every node answers: can I take this now? The standby system adds urgency: limited slots close fast.
05
Liquidity Layer
Standby system — fill rate stays high
When a creator accepts and then cancels, or a campaign goes unfilled, the standby pool activates. Standby-eligible creators (Rising tier and above) get real-time alerts. Merchants get filled slots. Creators get access to opportunities they might have missed. The mechanism keeps supply-demand liquid even at small scale.
