PROJECT INTRODUCTION

Push is infrastructure, not a marketplace.

Most platforms help merchants and creators find each other. Push makes the collaboration quantifiable, accountable, and worth doing again. That's a different business entirely.

SCROLL
ROOT CAUSE

The same broken system hurts both sides.

🏪 MERCHANT PROBLEMS
Follower count is a useless signal
Merchants evaluate creators by followers, aesthetics, and "vibes." None of these predict whether a creator will show up, post on time, or actually drive real customers.
Root cause: no creator credit system for local commerce
ROI is invisible
After spending $150 on a creator, merchants can't answer: did anyone actually come because of that post? Was the campaign worth it? Who should I work with again?
Root cause: no attribution layer
Every collaboration is manual
Outreach, negotiation, content approval, scheduling, reminders, proof tracking, payments — all managed through DMs and spreadsheets. For a cafe owner running their own business, this is unsustainable.
Root cause: creator collaboration treated as ad hoc
No protection when things go wrong
Creators take free product and disappear. Content goes up and then gets deleted. No-shows happen with no consequence. There's no escrow, no dispute path, no accountability.
Root cause: no consequence-backed trust system
🎥 CREATOR PROBLEMS
No pricing power
Rising creators cannot prove their commercial worth. Every merchant offers whatever they feel like, and without a verifiable track record, creators negotiate from zero every single time. The market has no memory.
Root cause: no portable, verifiable performance history
Trapped in freebie loops
Most gigs offer free product in exchange for a post. There's no structured path from doing a free shoot for a café to getting paid $50 a campaign to being on retainer with premium brands. Platforms don't distinguish onboarding from growth.
Root cause: no career progression framework
Income is unpredictable
Random DMs, inconsistent payments, vague requirements that change after the work is done. Creators have no pipeline, no recurring relationships, no predictable income. Every month starts from scratch.
Root cause: no pipeline infrastructure for creators
No accountability on the merchant side
Merchants can reject work arbitrarily, change requirements mid-campaign, or simply never respond. Creators have no recourse. The power imbalance is structural.
Root cause: no bilateral accountability layer

STRATEGIC ARCHITECTURE

Five systems. One infrastructure.

01
Campaign OS
Merchant-side — goal to structured campaign
Merchants pick a goal. The system generates a complete campaign brief. No blank-slate creation — choose 'boost weekday traffic' or 'launch new item' and the payout, creator requirements, and verification method are all pre-filled. Bad briefs no longer happen because someone didn't know what to write.
02
Creator Credit
For merchants and creators — performance history as currency
Every campaign updates the score. Completion rate, reliability, content quality, merchant satisfaction — high scorers unlock better campaigns and higher payouts. Scores decay over time — long inactivity costs you points. Performance, not followers.
03
Attribution
Platform-wide — did the campaign actually work?
5 verification layers: ① visit proof (timestamp, merchant confirmation) ② content live (URL, screenshot, persistence check) ③ engagement data (directional only) ④ merchant-side action (redemptions, bookings, visit tags) ⑤ repeat signal (merchant rebooking, creator invited to premium campaign). Each layer adds confidence.
04
Demand Surface
The live map — real-time opportunities
Creators see a commercial feed, not an explore page: distance, payout, slots remaining, deadline, tier required, is this worth taking right now. Every node drives action. Not browsing — deciding.
05
Liquidity Layer
Standby system — fill rate stays high
When a slot opens unexpectedly, standby-pool creators are notified immediately. Merchants don't scramble to fill. Creators don't refresh waiting. If you suddenly have free time, you can still land a good campaign.
