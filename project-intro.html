Product · The mechanism

Push: The Attribution Rail for Local Commerce

We prove which creator post drove which in-store purchase, so merchants pay creators for verified foot traffic instead of vanity metrics.

What it does

Six-step attribution mechanism.

01

Merchant signs up

Choose a tier: Attribution Lite (Free) / Essentials ($99/mo) / Pro Year 1 (5% of attributed revenue, cap $179/mo, floor $49/mo).

02

Creator posts

Creator posts about merchant on Instagram, TikTok, or Xiaohongshu — places they genuinely love.

03

QR code bound

Each post carries a Push QR code bound to creator × merchant × campaign.

04

Customer scans

Customer scans QR at checkout (or staff scans receipt).

05

Conversion recorded

Push records the conversion with three independent signals: QR scan + receipt timestamp + merchant confirmation.

06

Creator paid

Creator paid per verified conversion via Stripe Connect. Push takes rev share on payout.

What Push is not

Three clarifications.

Not a marketplace
We don't match creators to merchants. Creators post about places they love.
Not an agency
We don't manage relationships. We manage attribution data.
Not a platform
We don't rank by algorithm. We settle by verified conversion.
Why now

Four forces converging.

⚠

Cookie attribution is dead

iOS 14.5 (2021), Chrome 3P cookie deprecation (2024). Third-party attribution no longer works.

📍

No local creator rail exists

Despite a $250B creator economy globally, there's no attribution infrastructure for physical commerce.

⚖️

FTC compliance mandatory

FTC 16 CFR Part 255 actively enforced. Merchants need verified, disclosed, decaying disclosure records.

📱

QR is post-COVID native

No education required. Customers scan at checkout. Zero friction.

Current focus

Phase 0: Williamsburg Coffee+ Pilot

May–July 2026. 5–7 merchants, 8 Instagram micro-tastemakers + 5 Xiaohongshu creators. Gate: 10 verified attribution transactions, $1K creator payout, 90-day merchant retention ≥80%. If Phase 0 misses, we pause before Manhattan expansion.
