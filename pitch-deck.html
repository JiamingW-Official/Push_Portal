02 PROBLEM

Local creator marketing is broken on both sides.

MERCHANT
A café owner in SoHo wants to run a creator campaign. Here's what actually happens:
  • Posts a vague 'collab opportunity' on Instagram
  • Gets 20 DMs from creators with no way to compare them
  • Picks one based on follower count and aesthetic
  • Creator shows up, takes the free meal, maybe posts
  • No way to know if a single new customer came from it
  • Definitely won't know who to work with next time
$150 spent. Zero data. Zero repeatability.
CREATOR
A food creator in Brooklyn wants to build a real business. Here's what actually happens:
  • Spends hours researching local restaurants to DM
  • Gets ghosted by most, low-ball offers from the rest
  • Takes a free meal in exchange for a Reel
  • Gets great engagement but the restaurant doesn't care
  • Still no proof of commercial value for the next pitch
  • Still no path from 'free food' to '$200/campaign'
Great content. No credit. No career path.
"The market has abundant creator supply. It just isn't structured into a repeatable acquisition channel for local commerce."

Creators aren't media. They're unstructured customer acquisition channels.

What everyone else builds
  • Creator marketplaces (match and hope)
  • Influencer platforms (vanity metrics)
  • Discovery apps (exposure without measurement)
  • Brand deal tools (for big budgets, not local merchants)
What Push builds
  • Infrastructure layer (not a marketplace)
  • Structured campaign system (goal → verified outcome)
  • Performance credit (completion rate, not follower count)
  • Repeatable acquisition workflow (data feeds next campaign)
Push lets local businesses buy customer acquisition from rising creators through verified fulfillment and conversion history.

06 DEFENSIBILITY

The moat is proprietary credit data and switching cost.

MOAT 1
Creator Credit Capital (non-portable)

Every campaign builds a creator's Push Score: completion rate, merchant ratings, category expertise, geo-credit, dispute history. This data cannot be exported. A creator with 50 completed campaigns, 4.7 merchant rating, and Reliable tier status has built months of standing they cannot replicate elsewhere. Leaving means starting from zero.

MOAT 2
Merchant Operations Data (locked in)

Campaign history, creator performance benchmarks, ROI trends, automated re-booking — all platform-specific. A merchant who's run 10 campaigns on Push has a creator comparison dataset they cannot access anywhere else. Their best decisions live in the platform.

MOAT 3
Network-Locked Liquidity

Verified, scored, tier-ranked local creators only exist inside Push. Verified, performing local merchants only exist inside Push. Neither side can recreate this network off-platform.

07 LAUNCH STRATEGY

One neighborhood. Dense. Repeatable. Then expand.

PHASE 1 · WEEKS 1–4
Foundation
  • Select ONE dense F&B neighborhood (SoHo/LES/Williamsburg)
  • Onboard 5–6 committed merchants personally
  • Recruit 20–30 curated creators (local, mobile, reliable)
  • Build website MVP: campaign listings, proof flow, admin
  • Run 1–2 dry-run campaigns to refine the process
PHASE 2 · WEEKS 5–8
First Campaigns
  • 5 merchants × 2–3 creators = 10–15 slots
  • Daily ops: check proofs, confirm, pay within 24h
  • Track: fill rate, completion rate, merchant satisfaction
  • Target: ≥2 merchants repeat without being asked
PHASE 3 · WEEKS 9–12
Validate + Go Paid
  • Scale to 15 merchants (referrals + walk-in outreach)
  • Introduce creator scoring + standby notifications
  • Transition to paid: $99/mo or $49/campaign
  • Go/No-Go decision based on 7 metrics
SUCCESS TARGETS AT WEEK 12
40%+ merchant repeat rate 85%+ campaign completion <48hr avg time-to-fill 30%+ willingness to pay 25+ campaigns completed

Expansion without merchant repeat is fake progress. We don't expand until the first neighborhood works.
