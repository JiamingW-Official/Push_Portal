02 · What's broken

$165B in local ad spend — and merchants still can't prove a single creator post drove a store visit.

01

No attribution rail exists

Merchants pay Instagram, Yelp, Google, Mailchimp. None of them can prove which ad drove a physical store visit. The data simply doesn't exist.

02

Creators go unpaid for local impact

Local creators post about merchants every day and are almost never paid — because nobody can prove the post caused the visit. No proof, no paycheck.

03

No repeat, no channel

Without verified attribution there's no ROI. Without ROI there's no second campaign. The whole creator channel dies after one try.

03 · Key Insight

The attribution rail for physical commerce doesn't exist. We are building it.

Every part of the local commerce chain is broken for the same reason: there is no infrastructure from creator post to physical purchase that all three parties — merchant, creator, consumer — can trust. Push is that infrastructure.

04 · How It Works

Six steps, no magic. Every conversion is recorded, verified, and settled.

  1. Step 1
    Merchant signs up on Push
    Tier architecture: Attribution Lite (free acquisition loop) → Essentials ($99/mo) → Pro Year 1 (5% of attributed revenue, cap $179/mo, floor $49/mo). No setup fee.
  2. Step 2
    Creator posts with a Push QR code
    Each post carries a QR code bound to creator × merchant × campaign. Creator posts on Instagram, TikTok, or Xiaohongshu.
  3. Step 3
    Customer scans at the register
    Customer sees the post, visits the merchant, scans the QR code at checkout — or staff scans the receipt. The attribution event fires.
  4. Step 4
    Three signals verify the conversion
    QR scan + receipt timestamp + merchant confirmation. Three independent signals on one unified attribution_event record. Push records the conversion.
  5. Step 5
    Creator paid via Stripe Connect
    Creator is paid per verified conversion. Push takes a rev share on the payout. Merchant sees a weekly ROI card. Creator sees live earnings.
  6. Step 6
    All three sides settle on the same ledger
    Merchant, creator, and consumer records are unified. Push never holds money — all flows through Stripe Connect. No MSB/FinCEN exposure.
05 · Creator Passport

Creator reputation — portable, accumulated, non-negotiable.

Every verified conversion adds to a creator's reputation on Push. Six-tier system: Community (entry) → Explorer → Maker → Studio → Partner → Icon. Higher tiers unlock better campaigns, higher payout splits, and retainer access. Creators stay not because we lock them in — because we raise their $/conversion.

06 · Business Model

Three tiers. Outcome-based. Push earns when merchants get verified customers.

Essentials

$99 / mo

Always-on attribution, weekly ROI cards, creator roster management, campaign analytics dashboard. For merchants running creator marketing as a repeatable system.

Pro

5% of attributed revenue · cap $179/mo · floor $49/mo

Year 1 default. Outcome-based — merchants pay only when Push drives verified revenue. Auto-converts to $199 flat or $349 Advanced in Year 2+.

07 · Defensibility

Every verified conversion deepens the moat. Competitors can copy the product — not the attribution data.

M1 — Neighborhood Attribution Graph

Every merchant × creator × campaign combination generates a conversion record. After 1,000 events per neighborhood, the matching accuracy is irreproducible. It's a local intelligence asset that compounds.

M2 — Creator Productivity Lock

Creator reputation only accumulates on Push. Leave = lose your verified conversion history, tier status, and retainer access. The productivity cost of leaving compounds over time.

M3 — FTC-Compliant Attribution Records

FTC 16 CFR Part 255 requires verified, disclosed, decaying attribution records. Push provides these by design. Legacy platforms don't. Compliance becomes a switching cost.

10 · Risks

Three risks we state first, not last.

  • Phase 0 is the thesis. If 5–7 Williamsburg coffee shops don't show > 25% ROAS lift and > 80% retention by 2026-08, the Manhattan premium price point is unlikely to hold. We pause, re-scope, and tell investors. Defense: kill criteria are defined before launch.
  • A big incumbent (Meta, Google, Yelp) launches a free creator-attribution tool for local merchants. Medium probability Year 2–3. Defense: our neighborhood-level conversion graph and creator reputation data take years to replicate.
  • FTC guidance on creator attribution changes to require individual-level disclosure. Low probability. Defense: our attribution decay model and k ≥ 50 anonymization are designed to anticipate this.
