01 THE PITCH
Push: The Operating System for Local Creator Marketing
$165B is spent on local advertising every year. Almost none of it flows through creators — because there's no infrastructure to make it work. Until now.

Push turns one-off creator posts into repeatable, verified campaigns with real attribution. We're building the rails that make local creator marketing measurable — and scalable.

02 THE PROBLEM

$16B spent on creator marketing — and local businesses still can’t prove a single dollar came back.

MERCHANT
She spent $150 on a creator collab. She got a photo. Not a single trackable customer.
  • Posts ‘looking for food bloggers’ into the void — no filtering, no vetting
  • Gets 40 DMs: can’t tell a 500-view hobbyist from a 50K pro
  • Picks one by gut feel — follower count is the only ‘data’ she has
  • Creator ghosts twice, finally shows up, eats, leaves in 20 minutes
  • A Reel goes up 5 days late — no link, no promo code, no CTA
  • She’ll never know if even one person walked in because of it
$150 gone. No attribution. No learnings. She won’t try again.
CREATOR
He drove 12K views to a ramen shop. They didn’t even know his name.
  • Cold-DMs 30 restaurants — 26 ignore him, 4 say ‘sure, come eat’
  • No brief, no deliverables, no timeline — just ‘post something nice’
  • Spends 6 hours shooting + editing for a $25 meal
  • Reel hits 12K views — restaurant never checks, never reposts
  • No performance record he can show the next merchant
  • After 6 months: 50 free meals, zero paid deals
50 campaigns. $0 earned. No portfolio. No pricing power.
“There are 50 million creators worldwide. But for a restaurant on 7th Ave, finding one reliable creator who can actually drive foot traffic is still impossible.”
03 KEY INSIGHT

Every local merchant already pays for customer acquisition. They just can't buy it from the people who actually drive it — creators.

What everyone else builds
  • Creator marketplaces — match a name to a brand, then pray for ROI. No fulfillment layer, no feedback loop.
  • Influencer analytics — score creators by follower count, not by whether they actually drove a single customer through the door.
  • Discovery apps — generate impressions, but the merchant can't tell which visit came from which creator.
  • Brand deal platforms — minimum spend $5K+. A boba shop with $300/month doesn't exist in their world.
What Push builds
  • A fulfillment layer, not a marketplace — every campaign has a structured goal, a verified outcome, and a settlement.
  • Performance credit built on completion history — merchants pick creators the way they pick contractors: by track record.
  • Attribution at the visit level — the merchant knows exactly which creator drove which customer, and what it cost.
  • Works at $50/campaign — designed for the 12M+ local merchants that every other platform ignores.
The creator economy has a $250B supply side and no transactional infrastructure. Push is building the settlement layer — the system that turns an unstructured post into a priced, verified, repeatable unit of customer acquisition.

05 BUSINESS MODEL

Recurring + transactional + performance. Three revenue layers that compound — each one harder to churn out of.

LAYER 1
Merchant Subscription
$0 free (wk 1–8) → $99/mo Basic → $199/mo Pro

Predictable base revenue with near-zero churn friction. Once subscribed, merchants stop asking "is this campaign worth it?" — they just run more. Our data: subscribers launch 3x more campaigns than pay-per-use merchants.

LAYER 2
Campaign Service Fees
$39/campaign (Basic overage) · $29/campaign (Pro overage)

Revenue scales directly with value delivered. Every extra campaign means the merchant is getting results — and we get paid again. This layer has no ceiling: the better Push works, the more merchants spend.

LAYER 3
Performance Take Rate (future)
% of creator payout on premium campaigns

The holy grail of marketplace monetization — and only possible because Push owns the full loop from campaign creation to foot traffic attribution. Most platforms can't prove ROI; we can, so we earn a cut of the upside. This is where margins expand dramatically.

06 DEFENSIBILITY

Every transaction deepens the moat. Competitors can copy the product — not the data.

MOAT 1
Creator Credit Capital (non-portable)

Every campaign adds to a creator's Push Score — ratings, completion streaks, category expertise. A top-tier creator has months of reputation they can't export. Leaving means starting from zero on a platform where no one knows them.

MOAT 2
Merchant Operations Data (locked in)

Which creators convert? What ROI per category? When to re-book? Ten campaigns in, the platform knows their business better than they do. Switching means losing their best playbook.

MOAT 3
Network-Locked Liquidity

More creators attract more merchants. More merchants bring better pay, which attracts more creators. Each side makes the other harder to leave — and impossible to rebuild from scratch.

07 GO-TO-MARKET

We know exactly how to start — and we won't scale until the math works.

PHASE 1 · WEEKS 1–4
Density Before Scale
  • ONE dense F&B neighborhood — density creates word-of-mouth between merchants
  • 5–6 merchants onboarded personally — founder-led sales builds trust no ad can buy
  • 20–30 curated local creators — small, reliable pool outperforms a large flaky one
  • 1–2 dry-run campaigns — break the workflow before real money flows
PHASE 2 · WEEKS 5–8
Prove the Loop
  • 10–15 live campaign slots — enough volume to see real patterns
  • 24hr proof-to-pay cycle — speed is the product, not just a feature
  • Track fill rate, completion rate, merchant NPS — vanity metrics kill startups
  • The signal we're hunting: merchants repeat without being asked
PHASE 3 · WEEKS 9–12
Go / No-Go
  • Grow to 15 merchants — but only via referrals, proving organic pull
  • Activate creator scoring — surface quality, let the market self-correct
  • Transition to paid ($99/mo or $49/campaign) — willingness to pay is the only real validation
  • Hard Go/No-Go gate on 7 metrics — if we don't hit them, we pivot, not pretend
Week 12 validation milestones — every number is a kill switch
40%+ merchant repeat rate 85%+ campaign completion <48hr avg time-to-fill 30%+ willingness to pay 25+ campaigns completed

We'd rather kill a launch that doesn't repeat than scale one that does. Investor capital buys us the discipline to prove it right — or stop fast.
