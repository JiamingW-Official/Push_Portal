# PUSH — Pain Points and Root Cause Map Skill

## Purpose
This document identifies the deepest pain points in the Push ecosystem and distinguishes symptoms from structural causes.

The objective is to avoid superficial fixes and instead solve the actual bottlenecks that could prevent Push from becoming a durable business.

---

## 1. Merchant Pain Points

### Pain Point A: Cannot Judge Creator Quality
Merchants often evaluate creators by:
- followers
- aesthetics
- vibe
- sample past posts

These are weak decision signals for business outcomes.

#### Root Cause
There is no normalized merchant-readable credit layer.

#### Solution Direction
Build creator passport scoring that emphasizes:
- completion rate
- reliability
- merchant satisfaction
- verified campaign outcomes
- repeat suitability

---

### Pain Point B: Unclear ROI
Merchants cannot confidently answer:
- Did this creator bring real visits?
- Was this campaign worth it?
- Who should I work with again?

#### Root Cause
No structured attribution layer.

#### Solution Direction
Use multi-layer verification and compare outcomes at creator, campaign, and merchant cohort level.

---

### Pain Point C: Operational Friction
Small merchants lack time for:
- outreach
- negotiation
- approvals
- tracking
- reminders
- reconciliation

#### Root Cause
Creator collaboration is currently managed like ad hoc relationship work.

#### Solution Direction
Turn merchant collaboration into a structured operating flow.

---

### Pain Point D: Fear of Being Burned
Merchants worry that creators will:
- no-show
- publish low-quality content
- take free product and disappear
- overpromise and underperform

#### Root Cause
No consequence-backed trust system.

#### Solution Direction
Use credit scores, dispute logs, fulfillment states, and penalties where appropriate.

---

## 2. Creator Pain Points

### Pain Point A: No Pricing Power
Rising creators cannot prove what they are worth.

#### Root Cause
The market lacks a standardized local commercial credit system.

#### Solution Direction
Make performance history legible and convertible into better access and higher payout.

---

### Pain Point B: Income Instability
Creators face:
- random deal flow
- inconsistent payout
- low-quality offers
- heavy manual negotiation

#### Root Cause
No structured opportunity pipeline.

#### Solution Direction
Use the live demand surface, standby system, and tier-based progression to create more predictable opportunity flow.

---

### Pain Point C: Exploitative Low-End Offers
Creators often get trapped in “free sample” loops.

#### Root Cause
Platforms do not distinguish onboarding opportunities from true growth opportunities.

#### Solution Direction
Limit pure freebie pathways and make progress unlock meaningfully better economics.

---

### Pain Point D: Trust Asymmetry
Creators do not know:
- if merchants will pay fairly
- if requirements will change
- if campaigns are worth the effort
- if they are being judged fairly

#### Root Cause
Weak merchant accountability and weak platform mediation.

#### Solution Direction
Give merchants their own performance profile and track platform conduct.

---

## 3. Platform Pain Points

### Pain Point A: Cold Start
Without supply, merchants do not come. Without merchants, creators do not stay.

#### Root Cause
Two-sided market dependency.

#### Solution Direction
Start hyper-local and manually manufacture density.

---

### Pain Point B: Fraud
Fraud may take forms such as:
- fake engagement
- fake check-ins
- self-redemption
- creator-merchant collusion
- misleading proof

#### Root Cause
Weak verification and no anomaly detection.

#### Solution Direction
Layer proof systems and risk scoring.

---

### Pain Point C: Disintermediation
Creators and merchants may bypass the platform after first contact.

#### Root Cause
The platform is perceived as introduction-only rather than value-compounding.

#### Solution Direction
Make platform value persistent, not introductory.

---

### Pain Point D: Commoditization
Push could become “just another marketplace.”

#### Root Cause
If the company over-indexes on supply discovery and under-invests in credit, attribution, and workflow systems.

#### Solution Direction
Make proprietary data and workflow superiority central to the product.

---

## 4. Root Cause Framework

Every pain point in Push usually maps to one of five root causes. For risk mitigation responses, → See [Doc 04] for the defense strategies tied to each pain point.

1. Lack of structure
2. Lack of verification
3. Lack of repeatability
4. Lack of durable economic incentives
5. Lack of compounding platform data

The product should always solve root causes, not just visible frustrations.

---

## 5. Priority Pain Map

### Highest Severity
- weak merchant ROI confidence
- creator low retention
- off-platform leakage
- fraud and fake value
- shallow campaign repetition

### Medium Severity
- poor campaign design
- weak supply matching
- proof submission confusion
- slow approvals
- inconsistent creator quality

### Lower Severity
- social/community gaps
- aesthetic customization
- optional creative utilities not tied to economics

---

## 6. What Not to Misdiagnose

### Symptom: Creators are inactive
Possible real cause:
- low-quality opportunities
- unclear upward mobility
- too much friction
- weak standby value

### Symptom: Merchants do not repeat
Possible real cause:
- attribution too weak
- campaign templates too generic
- creator quality mismatch
- approval burden too high

### Symptom: Lots of signups but low transaction volume
Possible real cause:
- fake demand
- wrong wedge
- supply density too broad and weak
- the platform is attracting opportunists rather than reliable operators

---

## 7. Final Principle

The strongest businesses are not those with the fewest complaints.  
They are those that correctly identify which complaints are structural and build the business around resolving them.

Push should treat pain points as diagnostic signals for strategic design.
