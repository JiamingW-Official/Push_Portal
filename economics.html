Unit Economics & Revenue Modeling

How Push thinks about revenue quality, cost structure, and the path to a durable business model.

Deep dive into Push's economic model and pricing. For the strategic context, see Strategy Core.
Core Economic Principle

What Push Optimizes For

Not signups, campaigns created, map opens, or profile views. The north star is high-quality, repeatable, margin-positive demand routing.

Win by: merchant repeat rate, creator completion quality, transaction reliability, high-confidence attribution, and repeatable contribution margin.
Merchant Repeat Rate
Merchants return when platform value exceeds friction. Primary health signal.
Creator Completion Quality
Fulfillment without exception handling. Clean execution reduces operational overhead.
Transaction Reliability
End-to-end completion rate. A failed transaction costs more than it was worth.
High-Confidence Attribution
Verified proof of performance. Enables premium pricing and repeat buying decisions.
Contribution Margin Quality
Revenue that covers variable cost cleanly. The metric that determines if the business is real.
Repeatability
Systematic, not anecdotal. Each repeat cycle should cost less and produce more.
Revenue Architecture

5 Revenue Layers

Each layer has a distinct role. Layering order matters — later layers only work when earlier ones have proven reliability.

01
Merchant Subscription Revenue
STABILITY LAYER

The predictable foundation. Recurring revenue that funds baseline operations and reduces dependence on transactional volume fluctuations.

  • Campaign access & creation tools
  • Creator filtering & matching dashboard
  • Repeat campaign automation
  • Basic reporting & analytics
02
Campaign Service Fees
OPERATIONAL MONETIZATION

Per-campaign fees that capture value from the operational infrastructure Push provides: routing, workflow, settlement, reporting.

  • Creator routing & workflow structure
  • Proof collection & settlement
  • Campaign lifecycle reporting
03
Performance-Based Take Rate
STRONGEST LONG-TERM LAYER

The highest-margin, most defensible revenue. Fees contingent on verified completion, legible outcomes, and merchant-side success events. Requires attribution confidence before deploying.

  • Verified completion events
  • Payout size gating
  • Redemption outcome correlation
04
Creator Premium Revenue
OPTIONAL ACCELERATION ONLY

Only if it genuinely improves earning velocity, opportunity access, and payout timing. NOT a vanity subscription. Should not launch until the underlying value is demonstrable.

  • Priority pipeline access
  • Faster payout timing
  • Higher-quality campaign eligibility
05
Future Financial Products
POST-TRUST LAYER

Only after core transactional trust is proven. Premature financial product launch without operational credibility creates risk exposure without corresponding margin.

  • Payout acceleration
  • Creator cash advance
  • Insured settlement
  • Premium analytics
  • Chain-level reporting
Cost Structure

Variable vs. Fixed Costs

Understanding which costs scale with activity and which are structural determines what margin improvement requires.

Variable Costs
  • Payment processing
  • Fraud review & investigation
  • Support intervention
  • Merchant onboarding
  • Creator dispute resolution
  • Verification overhead
  • Local density activation
Fixed / Semi-Fixed Costs
  • Engineering & product development
  • Infrastructure & hosting
  • Account management
  • Operational tooling
  • Growth experimentation
What Good Unit Economics Looks Like
  • Fast campaign fill time
  • Low fraud review burden
  • Clear verification path
  • Limited creator / merchant confusion
  • Minimal manual intervention
  • Repeatable merchant behavior
  • Creator progression value
Stakeholder Economics

Why They Stay

Sustainable economics require both sides to perceive compounding value over time.

Merchant Economic Logic
Why merchants continue using Push
More structured, accountable, and repeatable than direct DM outreach or local agencies.
  • Legible acquisition cost
  • Superior creator quality vs. ad hoc
  • Low campaign setup friction
  • Strong repeat performance signals
  • Easier off-peak traffic management
Creator Economic Logic
Why creators continue using Push
Earnings become clearer, more merit-based, and more cumulative on platform.
  • Improving deal flow quality over time
  • Performance-gated access to better campaigns
  • More predictable payout timing
  • Merchant quality filtering
  • On-platform earnings exceeding alternatives
Diagnostic Framework

7 Contribution Margin Questions

The questions that reveal whether unit economics are improving or deteriorating. Ask these before and after every significant operational change.

  1. How much manual work does one campaign require?
  2. Which campaign types create the highest operational burden?
  3. Which merchant verticals generate the cleanest repeats?
  4. Which creator cohorts produce the lowest support load?
  5. What percent of campaigns require exception handling?
  6. Which workflows reduce human intervention over time?
  7. Which creator cohorts progress to higher-value work without additional support cost?
Risk Patterns

4 Economic Traps

Each trap looks like progress on surface metrics while underlying economics deteriorate. Recognizing them early is the work.

TRAP A High Gross Activity, Low Net Value Many signups and campaigns created, but low merchant repeat and heavy manual support. Volume without retention and quality signals a broken model.
TRAP B Premium Revenue Without Real Utility Charging creators before proving that premium access actually improves their outcomes. Short-term revenue, long-term trust erosion and churn.
TRAP C Weak Attribution, Aggressive Pricing Taking performance fees before the system can reliably verify performance. Creates merchant distrust and attribution disputes that undermine repeat behavior.
TRAP D Excessive Subsidy Dependence Relying on giveaways or deep discounts to prop up retention without underlying economics improving. Masks the real state of the business.
Development Roadmap

PHASE 01

  • Merchant repeat behavior
  • Workflow reliability
  • Clean vertical selection
PHASE 02
Efficiency: Fees & Quality
  • Campaign fee consistency
  • Creator quality differentiation
  • Support efficiency improvement
  • Basic dashboard utility
PHASE 03
Scale: Premium & Expansion
  • Better take-rate logic
  • Premium tier economics
  • Optional financial products
  • Multi-location merchant expansion
Final Standard

The Compounding Logic

If unit economics are working, each cycle of usage should make the next cycle cheaper and more reliable.

Every repeated merchant becomes cheaper to serve. Every high-quality creator becomes more valuable to route. Every clean campaign improves future matching and margin quality. PUSH — Unit Economics Standard
