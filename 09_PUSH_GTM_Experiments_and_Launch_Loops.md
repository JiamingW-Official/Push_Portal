# PUSH — GTM Experiments and Launch Loops Skill

## Purpose
This document defines how Push should launch intelligently, test wedges, and create real market feedback loops instead of vanity growth.

The objective is not mass awareness.
The objective is to discover a repeatable and defensible entry path.

---

## 1. Launch Philosophy

Push should not launch as a broad platform.  
It should launch as a concentrated market experiment.

The first goal is not scale.  
The first goal is to prove:
- merchants repeat
- creators progress
- campaigns can be fulfilled cleanly
- local density changes behavior

---

## 2. Initial Wedge Selection Criteria

A strong first wedge has:
- clear local demand
- visual creator compatibility
- frequent consumer purchase behavior
- manageable campaign complexity
- straightforward fulfillment
- merchant openness to experimentation

Likely strong wedges:
- cafes
- dessert shops
- beauty appointments
- boutique fitness intros
- small events or openings

---

## 3. Hyper-Local Launch Model

Start with one small zone where:
- merchants are dense
- creators can travel easily
- standby has real meaning
- map value becomes visible quickly

Example launch pattern:
- one neighborhood
- 10 merchants
- 30 curated creators
- a clear campaign density target

---

## 4. Early GTM Experiments

### Experiment A: Merchant Pain Discovery
Goal:
- identify strongest merchant use case

Method:
- interview and onboard 10 merchants
- compare which campaign objective gets strongest enthusiasm
- track whether merchants care more about traffic, launch, fill, or social proof

### Experiment B: Creator Supply Quality Test
Goal:
- identify which creator cohort is most reliable

Method:
- compare students, niche local creators, beauty creators, food creators, and event-focused creators
- track response speed, completion rate, and quality

### Experiment C: Standby Utility Test
Goal:
- see whether real-time slot refill changes creator engagement and merchant confidence

Method:
- create controlled slot release moments
- measure fill speed and conversion to completion

### Experiment D: Merchant Repeat Trigger Test
Goal:
- identify what actually causes merchants to run a second campaign

Method:
- compare merchants who receive post-campaign recommendations vs those who do not
- compare reporting depth effects on repeat intent

---

## 5. Launch Loops

### Loop 1: Merchant Outcome Loop
Better campaign outcomes
→ stronger merchant trust
→ repeat campaign
→ more demand density
→ better creator opportunity flow

### Loop 2: Creator Progression Loop
Better completion
→ stronger score
→ better access
→ higher payout opportunities
→ stronger creator retention

### Loop 3: Data Advantage Loop
More completed campaigns
→ better performance comparison
→ better matching
→ higher repeat rate
→ stronger platform quality

---

## 6. GTM Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake A: Overbroad Geography
A wide launch kills density.

### Mistake B: Overbroad Supply
Attracting too many weak creators lowers merchant trust.

### Mistake C: Weak Merchant Education
If merchants do not understand campaign purpose, results will disappoint.

### Mistake D: PR Before Repetition
Awareness without repeatability creates shallow traction.

---

## 7. What to Measure in GTM

Important launch metrics:
- merchant activation rate
- merchant repeat intent
- campaign fill speed
- campaign completion rate
- creator second-campaign rate
- standby response rate
- time-to-first-value for merchants
- time-to-first-earning for creators

These are better than pure signup growth.

---

## 8. Founder-Led Sales Approach

Early merchant acquisition must be direct, consultative, and focused on diagnosing demand problems rather than pushing features.

→ See [Doc 05] for the detailed merchant acquisition playbook, founder sales cadence, and conversation frameworks.

Core principle: Frame Push as a structured operating system for local customer acquisition, not as “influencer exposure.” Create the first win quickly to build momentum and credibility.

---

## 9. Expansion Logic

Only expand geography or category when:
- merchant repeat exists
- creator quality remains strong
- standby remains meaningful
- operations stay controlled
- campaign outcome quality is stable

Expansion without these signals is likely fake progress.

---

## 10. Final Standard

A strong GTM strategy for Push is one where:
- each launch cohort teaches the platform something real
- growth emerges from repeated value, not novelty
- density changes behavior for both creators and merchants
- the first neighborhood becomes a blueprint for the next one
