Creator Retention & Anti-Freeloading Defense

If creators grab free samples and leave, Push has no supply. If there is no supply, there is no marketplace. This is the single most dangerous failure mode for the entire business.

The Problem: Why Creators Leave

Three structural reasons cause creators to churn after initial onboarding. None of them are about the product being bad -- they are about the platform failing to feel like a career tool.

🎫
Looks Like a Coupon App
Perception
If the first experience is 'sign up, get free food,' creators mentally categorize Push as a coupon app. They use it until free stuff runs out, then leave. The positioning is broken before the product even launches.
👁
No Visible Long-Term Benefit
Motivation
Creators cannot see how staying on the platform makes their future better. There is no visible trajectory from 'free samples' to 'real income.' Without a clear growth path, rational creators leave to find direct brand deals.
📈
No Accumulation Effect
Structural
Nothing a creator does on Push builds up over time. No reputation, no history, no score, no credit. Every campaign is a fresh start. If platform activity does not compound, there is zero switching cost -- leaving costs nothing.

The Four Layers of Retention

Retention is not one mechanism. It is a stack of four layers, each stronger than the last. The bottom is necessary but weak. The top is what makes creators stay for years.

Layer 4 -- Strongest
Earning Efficiency
Creators earn more money, faster, and more reliably on Push than anywhere else. Higher-tier creators get priority matching, lower platform fees, guaranteed minimums, and faster payouts. The platform becomes the highest-ROI use of their time.
Retention power: Very High
Layer 3 -- The Key
Sunk Cost Assets (Platform-Internal Credit Capital)
The creator builds up platform-specific assets that cannot be exported. This is the single most important retention layer. If a creator leaves, they lose all of it.
  • Fulfillment history and completion rate
  • Merchant ratings and rebook score
  • Redemption and conversion performance
  • Geo-credit (local neighborhood authority)
  • Category credit (cuisine/vertical expertise)
  • Repeat booking score
  • Response speed and reliability score
  • Dispute-free history
Retention power: Critical
Layer 2 -- Growth Driver
Growth Feeling
Tier upgrades unlock real earning power. Creators can see themselves leveling up: New to Rising to Reliable to Elite. Each tier is visibly better. The next tier is always in sight.
Retention power: Moderate
Layer 1 -- Foundation
Instant Incentives
Free samples, sign-up bonuses, first-campaign rewards. Necessary for onboarding, but the weakest retention layer. If this is all you have, creators leave immediately after the incentive is consumed.
Retention power: Weak

Anti-Freeloading Mechanisms

Four interlocking mechanisms that prevent creators from treating Push as a free sample dispenser.

🎫
1. Sample Campaigns = Limited Entry Tickets
Gate
New creators get a limited number of pure-sample campaigns (not unlimited). These are entry tickets, not the destination. To unlock more campaigns, creators must hit quality thresholds on their initial ones.
  • Max 3 sample-only campaigns for new creators
  • Must complete on time with proof to count
  • Minimum merchant rating of 3.5 to unlock next tier
  • No-shows or late submissions burn entry tickets permanently
⭐
2. High-Tier Value Must Be Extremely Real
Pull
The gap between Tier 1 and Tier 3+ must be enormous and obviously valuable. This is what makes creators aspire to climb instead of leaving.
  • Paid gigs (not just free samples)
  • Recurring deals with proven merchants
  • Premium campaign pool access
  • Priority standby for high-demand campaigns
  • Guaranteed minimum payouts
  • Faster payouts (3 days vs 14 days)
  • Lower platform commission
🔐
3. History Becomes a Scarce Pass
Lock-in
Platform history data becomes the key that unlocks high-value opportunities. This cannot be faked, purchased, or built anywhere else. It takes months to accumulate.
  • 90-day completion rate (rolling window)
  • Merchant satisfaction scores
  • Dispute rate (lower = better access)
  • Category performance (proven verticals)
  • Local activity density (neighborhood expertise)
⚠️
4. Loss Aversion Design
Stick
People are 2x more motivated by fear of losing something than by gaining something equivalent. Design around what creators will LOSE if they stop being active.

Why the Flywheel Works
Each revolution of the flywheel makes the next revolution easier. A creator with 20 completed campaigns, a 95% completion rate, 4.7 merchant rating, and Reliable tier status has built something that took months. They cannot replicate it on any competing platform. They will not throw it away over a slightly better one-time offer elsewhere.

Critical Mistakes to Avoid

These four mistakes have killed retention strategies at other creator platforms. Push must avoid every single one.

1
Treating Perks as Retention
Free swag, birthday messages, 'creator appreciation week' -- none of this is retention. Retention is structural. Perks are decorative. If your retention plan is 'be nice to creators,' you will lose them to whoever pays more.
2
Making Tiers Too Easy to Game
If creators can reach top tier by completing 5 easy campaigns with friendly merchants, the tier is meaningless. Tier must require genuine performance across multiple dimensions: completion rate, merchant satisfaction, content quality, reliability.
3
Creator Platform Assets Too Weak
If the only thing a creator 'owns' on the platform is a profile photo and a follower count, there is nothing to lose by leaving. Platform assets must be rich: fulfillment history, merchant scores, category credit, geo-authority, reliability rating.
4
Adding Friction Without Benefit
Every rule, requirement, or process that does not directly increase creator earning power or reputation is pure friction. Creators tolerate friction only when the payoff is obvious. 'Upload proof within 48 hours' is fine if tier progression depends on it. 'Fill out a 20-field form' for no visible reward will kill onboarding.
