Root Causes

Why Creators Leave

Most creator churn isn't random. It's predictable, driven by three structural perception failures that compound over time. If we don't address these by design, the platform will hemorrhage its best creators.

01
Coupon App Perception
Creators perceive PUSH as a one-time free sample source, not a professional ecosystem. If the platform doesn't signal career seriousness, they won't invest serious time in it.
02
No Visible Long-Term Benefit
Creators who can't see a concrete path to better earnings, higher tiers, or exclusive campaigns will default to platforms that show them a clear upside trajectory.
03
Zero Accumulation Effect
If a creator's reputation, history, and ratings on PUSH don't compound into tangible advantages, there's no switching cost. No switching cost means no loyalty — only availability.

System Design

Four Anti-Freeloading Mechanisms

Structural design decisions — not policies — that make one-time extraction impossible and long-term commitment naturally rewarding.

🎫
Sample Campaigns = Entry Tickets

New creators get maximum 3 sample campaign slots. Each one requires hitting quality thresholds to unlock the next. No-shows or failures burn tickets permanently — and burn them publicly.

3 max → quality gate → unlock more access
⭐
High-Tier Value Must Be Extremely Real

Tier status must come with concrete, verifiable advantages that creators can feel every single time they log in — not just vague prestige.

  • Paid gigs and recurring brand deals
  • Priority campaign matching queue
  • Premium campaigns unavailable to lower tiers
  • Faster payouts (3-day vs 7-day)
  • Lower platform commission fees
  • Dedicated account support access
🔑
History Becomes a Scarce Pass

90-day completion rate, merchant ratings, dispute-free record, and geo-category credit build into a platform passport that grants access to top-tier campaigns. This cannot be faked, purchased, or built elsewhere in months.

Months of history → irreplaceable asset → real switching cost
⚠️
Loss Aversion Design

People fear loss 2x more than equivalent gain. Design what creators actively LOSE when they go inactive — make the loss concrete, visible, and emotionally salient.

Failure Modes

Mistakes That Will Destroy Retention

These aren't edge cases. They're the default failure modes of every creator marketplace that didn't think deeply enough about the retention architecture.

Mistake 01
Treating Perks as Retention

Discounts, badges, and referral credits feel like retention strategy but they're not. Perks are instantly copyable by any competitor. Real retention requires structural switching costs — assets the creator cannot take with them.

Mistake 02
Making Tiers Too Easy to Game

If creators can reach Elite tier in 2 weeks with low-quality completions, the tier system loses all credibility. Tiers must be hard to achieve, meaningful when reached, and visibly worth protecting once earned.

Mistake 03
Creator Platform Assets Too Weak

If a creator's history and ratings on PUSH don't unlock dramatically better opportunities, there's no reason to stay. The platform passport must be a meaningful key — not a participation trophy.

Mistake 04
Adding Friction Without Benefit

Verification requirements, strict policies, and compliance rules that aren't matched by commensurate earnings and access feel punitive, not professional. Every friction point must be justified by a visible, concrete reward.
