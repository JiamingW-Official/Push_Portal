Fraud & Integrity Operations

How Push preserves a trusted commercial environment while keeping friction minimal.

Integrity is not a policing function — it is part of the product itself.

Deep dive into Push's fraud prevention and platform integrity. For the broader risk landscape, see Risk Defense.
PHILOSOPHY

Goal: Preserve Trust, Not Punish

The goal is NOT aggressive policing. The goal is to preserve a trusted commercial environment while minimizing false positives and operational drag.

Fraud will emerge from opportunistic creators, weak merchant oversight, collusion, system loopholes, and incentive misalignment. The platform's response must be structural — not reactive, not punitive by default.

THREAT MODEL

Five Types of Fraud

Distinct failure modes requiring distinct detection and enforcement approaches.

01
CREATOR-SIDE
Fake Fulfillment
Creator claims they visited, completed, or posted content — but did not truly satisfy the campaign requirement. Proof may be fabricated, edited, or submitted from a past session.
Detection: Photo verification with EXIF metadata check, GPS check at submission time, timestamp cross-referencing against campaign window.
02
MERCHANT-SIDE
Fake Merchant-Side Validation
Merchant confirms weak or false completion for convenience or collusive reasons — approving work that does not meet campaign standards, reducing accountability and diluting platform quality.
Detection: Approval pattern analysis against platform norms, merchant-creator pair monitoring, approval rate deviation alerts.
03
REDEMPTION-SIDE
Fake Redemption
Offer usage inflated through self-redemption, friend loops, non-genuine visits, or repeated abuse of limited-use offers. Distorts campaign performance data and wastes merchant budget.
Detection: Device fingerprinting, location clustering analysis, redemption frequency caps, friend-graph overlap detection.
04
METRIC-SIDE
Engagement Inflation
Creators present high vanity metrics — follower counts, likes, reach — with little true campaign value. Purchased followers, fake interactions, or inflated reach claims misrepresent actual influence.
Detection: Engagement-to-follower ratio benchmarking, historical metric trend analysis, audience authenticity scoring.
05
COORDINATION
Collusion
Merchant and creator coordinate to game payout, rating, or campaign status — mutually benefiting from fraudulent completion cycles at the expense of platform integrity and fair competition.
Detection: Graph analysis of merchant-creator relationships, mutual approval pattern flagging, payout velocity anomaly detection.
DETECTION

Integrity Risk Signals

Behavioral patterns and data anomalies the platform monitors continuously — not as triggers for automatic punishment, but as inputs for risk scoring.

Sudden result spikes on a campaign after extended inactivity
Proof patterns that repeat unnaturally across submissions
Same-device or same-location redemption anomalies
Suspicious redemption timing relative to campaign open window
Merchant-creator pairs with abnormal mutual approval patterns
Creators with high completion rates but weak merchant-reported value
Creators who selectively target low-verification campaigns only
Engagement metrics inconsistent with historical account behavior
ARCHITECTURE

Fraud Prevention by Design

Structural mechanisms that reduce fraud opportunity without creating friction for honest users. Prevention is built in — not bolted on.

Milestone-Based Workflow
Campaigns progress through defined checkpoints, not a single pass/fail judgment. Each milestone is independently verifiable.
Campaign-Specific Proof Requirements
Each campaign type specifies what counts as valid proof — location check-in, content screenshot, engagement screenshot, merchant scan — reducing ambiguity and gaming.
Selective Verification Depth
Verification intensity is calibrated to campaign payout size and creator risk score — not applied uniformly. High-payout or flagged campaigns receive deeper review.
Creator Credit Impact
Integrity status affects campaign access, payout timing, and tier eligibility — making a clean record economically valuable and fraud economically costly.
Merchant Accountability
Merchant validation is tracked and weighted. Approval patterns that diverge significantly from platform norms surface as merchant integrity signals — creating feedback both ways.
Delayed Settlement Where Needed
Payouts for flagged campaigns or high-risk creators are held pending review. Honest users typically proceed without delay; settlement holds apply selectively.
Anomaly-Triggered Review, Not Universal Manual Review
Manual review is expensive and creates friction for everyone. Reviews are triggered by anomaly scores, not by default — preserving platform efficiency and user experience.
REVIEW SYSTEM

Three Review Tiers

Not all campaigns require the same scrutiny. Review depth scales with risk signals, not with payout alone.

TIER WHEN IT APPLIES PLATFORM ACTION
PASSIVE Low-risk campaigns, trusted creators, no anomaly signals detected Automated processing proceeds. No additional friction. Standard proof submission accepted.
TRIGGERED One or more risk signals appear; campaign enters elevated monitoring state Additional proof may be requested. Settlement may be delayed. Pair flagged for pattern monitoring.
MANUAL Payout disputes, repeated flags, suspected collusion, merchant complaints with evidence Human review assigned. Payout held. Both parties notified. Decision logged with rationale.
ENFORCEMENT

Consequence Framework

Rule-based consequences that are explainable, consistent, and proportional. Every consequence must pass a proportionality test.

CONSEQUENCE WHEN APPLIED INTENDED EFFECT
Warning First anomaly, ambiguous proof, borderline lateness Signals that behavior is being monitored; no economic impact
Score Reduction Confirmed weak proof, late submission with weak justification Reduces access to premium campaigns; creates economic signal
Tier Downgrade Repeated score reductions; consistent integrity pattern violations Removes access to higher-payout campaigns; status visible to merchants
Payout Delay Triggered review status; pending investigation Creates time buffer for investigation without permanent harm
Campaign Ineligibility Confirmed proof fabrication, confirmed redemption abuse Removes ability to participate in new campaigns; economic consequence
Temporary Suspension Pattern of confirmed fraud; second major violation Platform access removed for defined period; strong deterrent signal
Permanent Removal Confirmed collusion, repeated suspension, systematic fraud Permanent removal from platform; reserved for worst cases only
MERCHANT CONTROLS

Merchant Integrity Controls

Merchants are participants in the integrity system, not just victims of fraud. Merchant behavior affects the entire creator supply and campaign market.

→
Bait-and-Switch Demands
Merchant changes deliverable expectations after creator accepts — adding requirements, changing content standards, or demanding revisions outside campaign scope. Tracked as merchant integrity violation.
→
Unfair Rejections
Merchant rejects valid work without clear justification. Rejection rate monitored relative to platform norms. Systematic unfair rejection creates platform-level creator churn.
→
Refusing Settlement
Merchant delays or refuses payout for confirmed completed work. Platform enforcement step: settlement may be processed directly from merchant escrow with merchant integrity flag.
→
Manipulating Proof Standards Post-Acceptance
Retroactively raising proof bar after campaign launched to avoid paying creators — a deliberate integrity violation with direct economic impact on creators.
Merchant Integrity Affects
Creator visibility to their campaigns Ability to attract strong creators Support priority level Future campaign quality routing
DISTINCTION

Fraud vs. Mistake

This distinction matters for both fairness and retention. Treating mistakes as fraud destroys trust. Treating fraud as mistakes enables gaming.

MISTAKE / CONFUSION
Creator misunderstood campaign requirements
Proof submitted in wrong format or wrong step
Lateness due to schedule conflict or emergency
Campaign bad fit identified after acceptance
Weak content execution but genuine effort
INTENTIONAL FRAUD
Fabricated proof submitted knowingly
Repeated gaming of the same system loophole
Coordinated abuse with another account or merchant
Self-redemption of creator-initiated offers
Deliberate engagement inflation for campaign access
FINAL STANDARD

Protect merchants without crushing creator trust. Discourage abuse without overburdening good users. Create consequences that matter economically. Make the platform safer than informal alternatives.

Integrity is not just protection — it is part of the product.
