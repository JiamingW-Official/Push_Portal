Five Irreplaceable Platform Values
What the platform provides that private deals never can
1
Screening & Matching Efficiency
  • Smart recommendations based on category, location, and history
  • Auto-fill and standby fill when creators cancel
  • Target-based filtering by engagement, tier, and niche
  • Multi-campaign management from a single dashboard
Platform manages a creator acquisition program, not just introductions
2
Attribution & Data
  • Creator performance comparison across campaigns
  • Category analytics and trend insights
  • Campaign ROI tracking with cost per redemption
  • Fill rate, completion rate, and no-show analytics
Platform is a performance dashboard, not just a middleman
3
Payment, Dispute & Fulfillment Protection
  • Payout protection with escrow-style settlement
  • Proof flow: upload, review, confirm, settle
  • Dispute resolution with platform mediation
  • Cancellation rules, no-show penalties, and creator ratings
Walking the platform is safer for both sides
4
Continuous Supply & Rotation
  • Private deals only solve one collaboration at a time
  • Platform provides ongoing creator discovery and rotation
  • Fresh faces keep content authentic and prevent fatigue
  • Seasonal and category-specific supply matching
Merchants bypass for one deal; platform delivers an ongoing channel
5
Merchant Reputation System
  • Payment speed and reliability scoring
  • Communication quality and responsiveness
  • Promise fulfillment rate and creator satisfaction
  • Repeat booking rate signals merchant quality
Good merchants attract better creators; bad merchants get filtered out
Five Anti-Bypass Mechanisms
Structural incentives that make staying on-platform the rational choice
1
Future opportunities only exist on-platform
The next campaign, the next high-paying merchant, the next tier-up -- all only accessible through the platform. Bypassing today means missing tomorrow.
2
Private deals don't inherit platform credit
Creator Passport scores, merchant reliability ratings, and accumulated trust only exist on-platform. Off-platform deals start from zero every time.
3
On-platform re-booking is easier than DMs
Why negotiate via DM when you can click one button?
One-click: duplicate campaign, invite same creator, auto-set payout, auto-collect proof
4
High-tier benefits only settle on-platform
Premium payouts, priority matching, exclusive campaigns, and bonus multipliers are tied to platform transactions. Private deals can't unlock these rewards.
5
Light rules, not heavy bans
Don't punish contact -- incentivize transactions. The platform doesn't block DMs or ban users for talking privately. Instead, it makes the on-platform path so smooth that going off-platform feels like extra work.

The Ultimate Question

From Transaction Entry Point to Value Settlement Layer

Push isn't asking 'how to prevent freeloading' or 'how to prevent bypass'. The real question is: How does the platform evolve from 'transaction entry point' to 'value settlement layer'?

Only when Push becomes this settlement layer: Creators leaving lose credit and future earnings. Merchants leaving lose efficiency, data, and continuous supply.
🎭
Creator Loss if Leaving
Accumulated Passport score, tier status, priority matching, historical performance data, earnings multipliers, and access to premium campaigns -- all non-transferable.
🏪
Merchant Loss if Leaving
Campaign analytics history, creator performance benchmarks, automated re-booking workflows, dispute protection, continuous supply pipeline, and reputation score -- all platform-locked.
