(Strategy Core)

Positioning is a decision, not a description.

Push is the attribution rail for local commerce. We prove which creator post drove which in-store purchase, so merchants pay for verified foot traffic instead of vanity metrics. Everything we build — and everything we refuse to build — comes from that one choice.

(The choice)

Why Attribution Rail, not Agency / Platform / Marketplace

Attribution Rail (Push)
We are the proof layer — QR scan + receipt timestamp + merchant confirmation = one attribution event. Three independent signals, one ledger, Stripe Connect underneath.
NOT Agency
Agencies hold creator relationships. We hold attribution data — non-portable, compounding, moated.
NOT Platform
Platforms rank by algorithm. We settle by verified conversion. Different fundamental unit.
NOT Marketplace
Marketplaces match supply and demand. We prove that the match resulted in a physical visit.
(Agentic roadmap)

AI automation — Phase 0 first, then optionality.

  1. Phase 0
    Manual founder ops
    The founder manually acts as merchant advisor — reviewing ROI cards, suggesting creator mix, optimizing campaign timing. Phase 0 (2026) is all about landing verification, not automation.
  2. Year 2+
    AI Copilot (conditional on Phase 0 data)
    Once we have 90 days of verified, real-world transaction data from Phase 0 merchants, we can train prediction models for creator ROI, campaign timing, and merchant upsell. Automating this layer is a Year 2 product decision, not a Seed-stage priority.
(Strategic rules)

The non-negotiables.

  • Phase 0 data before any expansion decision. No silent drift from Williamsburg to Manhattan.
  • FTC 16 CFR Part 255 compliance is a moat, not a cost. Every attribution event is verified, disclosed, and decaying by design.
  • Push never holds money. Stripe Connect is the financial rail. This is a compliance moat against Square, Toast, Chime.
  • Three independent signals per conversion: QR scan + receipt timestamp + merchant confirmation. Honest about 95% decision confidence, not claiming 100% precision.
(Defensibility)

Three moats that compound.

  • M1: Neighborhood Attribution Graph — Each verified transaction trains a local preference model non-exportable to competitors. The data moat compounds with every scan.
  • M2: Creator Reputation Lock — Creator earnings and tier are tied to their Push attribution history. Reputation is non-portable. Churn destroys career capital.
  • M3: FTC-Compliant Attribution Records — We generate the verified/disclosed/decaying records that FTC 16 CFR Part 255 now requires. Legacy platforms don't. We do, by design.
