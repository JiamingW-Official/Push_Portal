The Core Thesis

Two Questions That Determine Everything

Before anything else, any investor, advisor, or serious operator will ask these two questions. If you can't answer them with conviction, the rest doesn't matter.

Q1
Why do creators stay long-term, instead of grabbing free samples and leaving?
Q2

The Danger

If Push is Just a Matching Tool

Three reasons why matching tools always fail.

01
🔓
Zero Switching Cost
If everything a creator does on the platform can be replicated elsewhere — or abandoned without loss — they will leave the moment a better offer comes. No lock-in means no loyalty. No loyalty means no business.
02
📊
No Network Effect
A simple matching tool gets better for no one as it grows. More users = more noise, not more value. Without compounding data and reputation layers, scale delivers diminishing returns — not defensibility.
03
💸
Commoditization Death
Any well-funded competitor can clone a matching interface in 3 months. Without structural lock-in, you get undercut on price and replaced. This is the graveyard of every gig platform that never built a moat.
