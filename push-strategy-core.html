The Core Thesis

Two Questions That Determine Everything

Before anything else, any investor, advisor, or serious operator will ask these two questions. If you can't answer them with conviction, the rest doesn't matter.

Q1
Why do creators stay long-term, instead of grabbing free samples and leaving?
Q2
Why don't merchants bypass the platform and deal directly with creators?
If you cannot answer both questions convincingly, Push is just a tool. Tools get replaced. Tools get commoditized. Tools die.
The Danger

If Push is Just a Matching Tool

Three reasons why matching tools always fail.

01
🔓
Zero Switching Cost
If everything a creator does on the platform can be replicated elsewhere — or abandoned without loss — they will leave the moment a better offer comes. No lock-in means no loyalty. No loyalty means no business.
02
📊
No Network Effect
A simple matching tool gets better for no one as it grows. More users = more noise, not more value. Without compounding data and reputation layers, scale delivers diminishing returns — not defensibility.
03
💸
Commoditization Death
Any well-funded competitor can clone a matching interface in 3 months. Without structural lock-in, you get undercut on price and replaced. This is the graveyard of every gig platform that never built a moat.

Investor Readiness

Retention & Disintermediation Defense

The chapter every serious investor will ask about. Answer these three questions strongly, and the project becomes fundable.

1
Question 01
What non-transferable assets do creators accumulate?
Answer: Passport scores, Tier benefits, earnings history, geo-credit, merchant ratings — none portable. A creator who leaves PUSH loses their entire professional identity on the platform.
See Creator Retention Architecture →
2
Question 02
What continuous value do merchants get that private deals can never provide?
Answer: Ongoing supply, data intelligence, automated workflows, dispute protection, quality-rated creator pool. A private deal provides none of this infrastructure — only a one-time transaction.
See Anti-Disintermediation Defense →
3
Question 03
Why is on-platform re-booking better in every dimension?
Answer: Faster, cheaper, safer, auto-settled, proof-verified, credit-building — vs. DMs, cash transfers, disputes, no records. The platform is not just easier; it is structurally superior in every measurable way.
If answered weakly, the project appears fragile. If answered strongly, the project becomes fundable.
