CORE MECHANISM

Standby is not a waitlist. It's Push's real-time slot routing engine.

Standby routes time-sensitive local demand to qualified creators in real time. It increases merchant fill rate, creator engagement, and platform liquidity — the mechanism that turns Push from an information board into a living market.

DEFINITION

What Standby actually is.

When a campaign has open slots, a creator drops out, or a merchant adds last-minute capacity — qualified creators in the standby pool get prioritized access to fill the gap.

Standby transforms uncertainty into dispatchable opportunity.

Local creator campaigns have inherent volatility: merchants can't always fill every slot perfectly, creators can't always apply at the right time, people cancel, no-show, or underperform. Without standby, every gap becomes a lost opportunity, slower execution, merchant distrust, and creator idleness.

WHY IT MATTERS

Push is a real-time supply-demand system. Standby is what makes it work.

Every campaign has a time window, a location, a slot count, and a specific goal. This creates natural liquidity problems that standby solves.

SCENARIO 1
Creator drops out last-minute
A cafe campaign has 5 creators. One cancels 12 hours before the deadline.
WITHOUT STANDBY
Merchant has to repost, re-recruit, re-negotiate. The slot likely goes unfilled.
WITH STANDBY
Platform instantly pushes the slot to qualified standby creators. Filled in 3 hours.
SCENARIO 2
Merchant wants to add more slots mid-campaign
A merchant sees good results and decides to add 2 more creator slots this week.
WITHOUT STANDBY
Has to create a new campaign from scratch. Loses momentum.
WITH STANDBY
Platform releases slots from the standby pool immediately. No friction.
SCENARIO 3
Creator happens to be nearby
A creator didn't apply originally, but is in SoHo today and sees a campaign slot about to close.
WITHOUT STANDBY
Missed opportunity. Creator can't enter late.
WITH STANDBY
Standby gives them a lightweight entry into the opportunity flow.
Standby upgrades Push from a “sign-up system” to a real-time dispatch system.
CORE LOGIC

Not first-come-first-served. Priority-based allocation.

1. Who can enter standby

Not everyone qualifies. The standby pool is gated, not open.

🎖️
Creator Tier
Higher tiers get standby access first. Partner and Closer see opportunities Explorer can't.
📍
Location Match
Proximity to the campaign location. Local creators get priority over distant ones.
📊
Performance History
Completion rate, reliability score, response speed, and merchant satisfaction.
🏷️
Category Fit
Creators with experience in the same F&B subcategory (cafes, boba, bakeries) get priority.
⚡
Recent Activity
Active creators in the last 30 days are prioritized over dormant ones.
⭐
Premium Status
Creators on merchant whitelists or with premium badges get additional priority weight.

2. When standby triggers

Regular applications are the standard supply. Standby is the emergency & acceleration supply.

Creator cancels
Slot unfilled near deadline
Merchant adds capacity
Fast-fill mode activated
Last 24-hour sprint

3. How allocation works

The platform decides who deserves the opportunity first, then lets them compete on speed.

Step Action Detail
1 Filter Only creators matching campaign requirements
2 Rank Sort by tier, score, proximity, response history
3 Push Notify top-ranked candidates first
4 Confirm First to confirm gets the slot
5 Cascade If no response within window, cascade to next tier
MERCHANT VALUE

For merchants: buy certainty.

📈
Higher fill rate
Fewer empty slots. Faster execution. More stable outcomes. Merchants' biggest fear — posting a campaign that doesn't fill — is eliminated.
🔄
Zero re-recruitment friction
Without standby, a single dropout means: re-post, re-communicate, re-review. With standby, the platform handles the swap automatically.
🤝
Builds trust in the platform
Merchant repeat depends on one question: “When something goes wrong, can the platform self-correct?” Standby is that self-correction.
Standby lets merchants buy certainty of execution, not just access to creators.
CREATOR VALUE

For creators: always another chance.

🎯
Higher opportunity density
Not selected in the main pool ≠ no chance. Last-minute slots = new opportunities. Being nearby = quick grab. This dramatically increases creator activity.
🔥
Natural FOMO & engagement
Only 2 slots left. Last 6 hours. Nearby cafe just released a slot. Standby makes creators feel like they're participating in a real market, not browsing a static board.
⬆️
Tier = real power, not just a badge
Higher tier = higher standby priority = faster access to better-paying campaigns. Creators directly feel: upgrading = earning more money today, not someday.

💧
Liquidity engine

📡
Real-time market feel
Without standby, Push is a static campaign board. With it, the platform has: fills, releases, countdowns, competition, push notifications, last-minute grabs — a living market.

Private deals solve one collaboration. Standby helps Push own the continuous supply advantage.

Push's standby is a real-time matching engine that routes time-sensitive demand to the most qualified creator.
RETENTION IMPACT

Standby is a retention multiplier.

🔄
Creates “come back and check” behavior
If the platform is a static feed, creators visit once and leave. Standby creates: there might be a slot right now, something nearby might open up, high tier might get an exclusive push. This increases return frequency.
💼
Transforms into job marketplace behavior
Creators aren't just making content — they're waiting for and competing for high-quality opportunities. This makes Push behave more like a job/marketplace than a content community.

Instant reward for progression
When upgrading tier visibly increases standby priority, progression isn't someday useful — it's different today. This turns the tier system from a badge into a revenue driver.
ANTI-BYPASS

Why standby makes bypassing Push irrational.

The biggest limitation of private merchant-creator deals is that they can't replicate what standby provides.

Private deals offer:
A fixed roster of 2–3 familiar creators. No backup. No expansion. No priority routing. If someone drops, you're on your own.
Push standby offers:
Continuous access to new backup creators. Fast fill on demand. Higher-tier availability at any moment. More flexible opportunity flow than any private arrangement. Dynamic supply control.
Standby helps Push platformize both “temporary gaps” and “instant supply” — the two things private deals can never solve.
