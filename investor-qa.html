15 Hard Questions.
Direct Answers.

The questions every investor asks about Push — from business model to moat to risk. No dodging.

I. Core Questions — They Will Ask These First
Q1
Why won't this become another generic marketplace?
Investors worry: all two-sided platforms end up with low quality + price wars + no differentiation.
Push is not an open marketplace — it is a performance-gated system. Creators must earn their way into higher tiers through verified execution. Merchants don't post freely — they run structured campaigns. High-value opportunities are allocated by tier, not listed openly. We control who gets access to high-quality demand.
We're building market structure, not a trading platform.
Q2
Why won't merchants bypass you?
Investors worry: merchants and creators will just work together directly.
If Push only introduced creators, it would be bypassed. We prevent this with three layers:

1. Credit system (non-portable) — Creator performance only accumulates on-platform. Leave = lose your history.
2. Workflow advantage — Campaign templates, creator management, one-click repeat. Faster inside than outside.
3. Opportunity control (most important) — Better creators, better campaigns, higher tier access. Off-platform = one-time deal. On-platform = continuously better opportunities.
We don't just enable transactions — we control future opportunities.
Q3
How do you solve cold start?
Investors worry: two-sided markets are the hardest to bootstrap.
We don't start as a marketplace. We start as a controlled environment.

One neighborhood (SoHo). One vertical (cafes). Hand-picked creators (not open registration). Founder-led merchant onboarding. The goal is not scale — it's density. 10 merchants, 50 creators, 12 weeks, $1,500 budget. Prove the unit before building the machine.
Q4
How do you ensure creator quality?
Investors worry: bad supply will destroy the platform.
Quality is enforced through structure, not moderation.

We use: tier progression, completion rate tracking, merchant feedback, and dispute tracking. Creators are ranked by execution ability, not follower count. Underperformers get demoted, lose campaign access, and eventually get removed. Quality is 25% of the scoring formula — bad content drops you fast.
Q5
How do you handle attribution?
Investors worry: “How do you prove a creator actually brought customers?”
We don't claim perfect attribution. We improve decision confidence.

We combine: visit proof, content proof, merchant confirmation, redemption signals, and repeat behavior. It's not 100% precise — but it's far more reliable than the current method (DM + guessing). What merchants actually care about is one question: “Should I do this again?”
II. Business Model
Q6
How do you make money?
Three revenue layers, all aligned with outcomes:

1. Merchant subscription — $79/month for platform access
2. Campaign fee — $29-39 per additional campaign
3. Performance-based take — 15% on verified completions

We only make money when the system delivers results.
Q7
Does unit economics work?
A typical campaign:

Merchant pays ~$200-300
Creator payout ~$120-180
Platform takes ~$40-60

Margin comes from campaign fees + take rate. But the real leverage is merchant repeat — acquisition cost is front-loaded, but repeat campaigns have near-zero marginal cost.
Q8
How do you define PMF?
When merchants run campaigns repeatedly without being pushed.

Key signals: repeat rate, time between campaigns, creator re-selection. If they come back on their own — it works.
If they come back on their own, it works.
III. Growth & Competition
Q9
Why now?
Three converging forces:

1. Creator supply has exploded — critical mass reached in 2024
2. Brands are shifting from impressions to conversion
3. Local spending is high-frequency, underserved by existing tools

Supply and demand both exist. The infrastructure between them doesn't.
Q10
Who are your competitors?
Not direct platform competitors — alternatives:

DM creators directly. Hire agencies. Run TikTok/Yelp ads.

The difference: they help you find creators. We help you operate them.
Q11
Why won't big platforms do this?
Because this is operationally heavy and locally fragmented.

Every city is different. Merchants are small and scattered. Creator behavior is complex. Big platforms do distribution. We do the execution layer — the messy, local, high-touch work that doesn't scale the way big platforms want to scale.
IV. Moat & Defense
Q12
What is your moat?
The moat is the performance data layer.

We accumulate: creator reliability scores, campaign effectiveness data, category insights. The more campaigns run, the better the matching, the harder to replicate. It compounds — and it can't be copied by launching a competing app.
Q13
Why won't it devolve into a low-quality market?
Because access is gated, not open.

Tier controls who gets what campaigns. Campaign structure prevents race-to-bottom pricing. Opportunity allocation is layered. This is not a free market — it's a curated system.
V. Risks — We Say It First
Q14
What is the biggest risk?
Four risks we actively manage:

1. Cold start density — solved by hyper-local launch (one neighborhood)
2. Creator quality — solved by tiered scoring + demotion
3. Attribution trust — solved by 5-layer verification
4. Disintermediation — solved by credit + workflow + opportunity control

We don't eliminate risk. We structure our way through it.
VI. The Ultimate Question
Q15
What are you actually building?
Push is building the infrastructure layer that turns creators into a reliable, repeatable acquisition channel for local businesses.

This is not a marketplace.
This is not influencer marketing.

This is the system that makes creator-driven demand measurable, repeatable, and scalable.
