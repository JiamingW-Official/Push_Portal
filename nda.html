Non-Disclosure Agreement

Influencer Research Interview — Confidential

This Non-Disclosure Agreement (this “Agreement”) is entered into as of the date of submission below (the “Effective Date”) by and between:

(1) Push, a project team operating under the direction of its founding members with the intent to incorporate as a legal business entity (the “Disclosing Party”); and

(2) The individual identified below (the “Receiving Party”, and together with the Disclosing Party, the “Parties”).

This Agreement shall remain valid and enforceable regardless of whether Push has formally incorporated at the time of execution and shall automatically transfer to and bind any successor entity formed by the Push founding team.

1.  Purpose

The Disclosing Party intends to share certain confidential information with the Receiving Party in connection with a research interview (the “Interview”) conducted for the purpose of gathering user insights, market feedback, and product validation related to the Push platform. The Interview is similar in nature to a focus-group session.

2.  Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” means any and all non-public information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party during or in connection with the Interview, whether disclosed orally, visually, in writing, or by demonstration, including but not limited to:

  • (a)  business concepts, strategies, plans, and product ideas;
  • (b)  product designs, prototypes, wireframes, and user-interface mockups;
  • (c)  technical architecture, algorithms, and software features;
  • (d)  business models, pricing strategies, and financial projections;
  • (e)  marketing strategies, go-to-market plans, and partnership arrangements;
  • (f)  any other information identified as confidential at the time of disclosure.

Confidential Information does not include information that: (i) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (ii) was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure; (iii) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or (iv) is lawfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

3.  Obligations of the Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall:

  • (a)  hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
  • (b)  not disclose, publish, or otherwise disseminate any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;
  • (c)  not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than participating in the Interview;
  • (d)  not reproduce, copy, or record any Confidential Information except as expressly authorized by the Disclosing Party;
  • (e)  exercise reasonable care to protect the secrecy and prevent unauthorized disclosure or use of the Confidential Information.

4.  Recording & Data Usage

The Receiving Party acknowledges that the Interview may be recorded (audio and/or video) for the Disclosing Party’s internal research purposes only. Recordings will not be published, shared externally, or used for marketing without the Receiving Party’s separate written consent. The Disclosing Party may use anonymized, aggregated insights derived from the Interview for product development, internal reporting, and strategic planning.

5.  No Obligation or Relationship

Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to enter into any further agreement, partnership, or business relationship. Participation in the Interview does not create an employment, agency, joint-venture, or partnership relationship between the Parties.

6.  Term

The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement shall survive for a period of two (2) years from the Effective Date, unless extended or shortened by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

7.  Remedies

The Receiving Party acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages alone would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

8.  Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, United States, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

9.  General Provisions

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous understandings, whether oral or written. No amendment or waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties. If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
