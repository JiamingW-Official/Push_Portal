# PUSH — Risk Register and Defense Playbook Skill

## Purpose
This document identifies the primary strategic, operational, marketplace, and behavioral risks facing Push and outlines practical responses.

The purpose is not to eliminate risk completely.
The purpose is to make risk legible, prioritized, and actively managed.

---

## 1. Strategic Risks

### Risk 1: Derivative Positioning
Push may be perceived as:
- a generic influencer marketplace
- a local discovery app
- a reward system
- a map layer with light creator features

#### Consequence
Weak investor conviction, weak moat narrative, easy dismissal.

#### Defense
Position Push as:
- local creator performance infrastructure
- merchant acquisition operating system
- creator credit and attribution layer

---

### Risk 2: Weak Wedge
Trying to launch across too many categories or neighborhoods.

#### Consequence
Low density, bad user experience, weak conversion.

#### Defense
Start with:
- one dense area
- one or two strong verticals
- founder-managed supply and demand

---

### Risk 3: Feature Drift
Adding low-value features that make the platform feel broad but weak.

#### Consequence
Bloat, confusion, slower execution, weaker company identity.

#### Defense
Prioritize only features that improve:
- merchant ROI clarity
- creator earning progression
- defensibility
- repeat transactions
- trust and verification

---

## 2. Marketplace Risks

### Risk 4: Cold Start Failure
Not enough merchants or creators in the initial zone.

#### Consequence
Low liquidity, poor experience, weak retention.

#### Defense
Create density manually:
- founder-led merchant acquisition
- curated creator onboarding
- concentrated campaign volume
- seeded standby opportunities

---

### Risk 5: Low Merchant Retention
Merchants try once and do not return.

#### Consequence
Weak revenue base and poor signal for product-market fit.

#### Defense
Improve:
- campaign template quality
- post-campaign reporting
- next-campaign recommendations
- creator quality sorting
- fill speed
- ROI legibility

---

### Risk 6: Low Creator Retention
Creators join for freebies, then disappear.

#### Consequence
Supply quality degrades and platform becomes exploitative or noisy.

#### Defense
Use:
- strong progression system
- limited freebie dependency
- clear earnings ladder
- standby utility
- high-value unlocks
- platform-native reputation capital

---

## 3. Behavioral Risks

### Risk 7: Disintermediation
Creators and merchants take relationships off-platform.

#### Consequence
Platform loses future revenue and data.

#### Defense
Make on-platform value superior through:
- credit accumulation
- repeat workflow automation
- visibility into new opportunities
- faster payouts
- standby access
- merchant campaign history
- trusted dispute resolution

---

### Risk 8: Opportunist Supply
Users join mainly to exploit free goods or low-effort incentives.

#### Consequence
Weak merchant trust and low transaction quality.

#### Defense
Gate better opportunities behind:
- fulfillment quality
- platform history
- reliability
- performance progression

---

### Risk 9: Merchant Abuse
Merchants may:
- over-demand
- delay approvals
- refuse fair payment
- shift expectations mid-campaign

#### Consequence
Creator churn and distrust.

#### Defense
Track merchant behavior and build merchant accountability into the system.

---

## 4. Fraud Risks

### Risk 10-12: Fake Proof, Fake Redemption, Engagement Inflation
Creators may submit misleading visit/content evidence, inflate outcomes through self-redemption or collusion, or use vanity metrics to appear effective.

#### Defense
Use timestamped uploads, geo checks, merchant verification, pattern analysis, and anomaly review. De-emphasize vanity metrics in scoring.

→ See [Doc 08] for the full fraud operations framework, detection patterns, and tier-based fraud defense strategy.

---

## 5. Operational Risks

### Risk 13: Approval Bottlenecks
Campaigns stall because merchants delay reviews.

#### Defense
Use SLA-style nudges, deadlines, and auto-routing support mechanisms.

---

### Risk 14: Settlement Delays
Creators lose trust if payout is slow or unclear.

#### Defense
Make settlement rules explicit, visible, and consistently enforced.

---

### Risk 15: Support Load Explosion
Manual issue resolution may overwhelm early operations.

#### Defense
Build exception-based ops rather than chat-based ops:
- overdue proof flags
- suspicious result flags
- dispute triggers
- campaign failure dashboards

---

## 6. Competitive Risks

### Risk 16: Platform Expansion by Large Players
TikTok, Yelp, or other platforms may add local creator tools.

#### Consequence
Push could be copied at the feature level.

#### Defense
Large platforms may copy surface features, but Push can defend through:
- focused vertical depth
- better workflow
- local campaign operating logic
- stronger creator credit layer
- better merchant repeat mechanics
- lower operational ambiguity

---

## 7. Risk Prioritization Framework

### Existential Risks
- poor merchant retention
- low creator quality retention
- disintermediation
- weak attribution
- cold start failure

### Major Risks
- fraud
- commoditization
- supply opportunism
- weak wedge selection

### Manageable Risks
- UI complexity
- optional feature prioritization errors
- moderate support inefficiencies

---

## 8. Final Defense Principle

Push does not win by preventing all risk.  
Push wins by making the platform more economically rational than off-platform behavior.

When that is true:
- good creators stay
- good merchants repeat
- weak actors are filtered out
- data compounds into advantage

That is the real defense system.
