# PUSH — Execution Skills Playbook

## Purpose
This document defines the core execution capabilities required to build Push successfully.

These are not generic startup skills.
They are the specific operating skills needed to turn the concept into a defensible company.

---

## 1. Merchant Acquisition Skill

### Objective
Acquire the right initial merchants, not just any merchants.

### Required Competence
- identify merchants with visible need for acquisition help
- spot businesses with visual/content-friendly offerings
- assess whether merchant operators are responsive and realistic
- sell a repeatable growth system, not a creator posting service

### Best Initial Targets
- cafes
- dessert shops
- beauty studios
- boutique fitness
- pop-up events
- opening-phase local brands

### What Good Looks Like
A strong merchant is:
- motivated
- operationally responsive
- willing to test repeatedly
- able to fulfill demand
- not expecting unrealistic viral outcomes

---

## 2. Creator Quality Sourcing Skill

### Objective
Recruit creators who can become reliable commercial operators.

### Required Competence
- identify rising creators with local relevance
- distinguish aesthetic quality from business reliability
- evaluate mobility, responsiveness, and consistency
- avoid attracting only freebie opportunists

### What Good Looks Like
A strong creator is:
- responsive
- geographically relevant
- capable of simple execution
- willing to follow structured deliverables
- motivated by progression, not just one-off perks

---

## 3. Campaign Design Skill

### Objective
Design campaigns that are attractive, measurable, and operationally realistic.

### Required Competence
- convert merchant objectives into campaign templates
- balance creator incentive with merchant ROI
- define realistic slot counts
- choose verification methods appropriate to the campaign
- avoid vague, style-driven campaign briefs

### What Good Looks Like
A strong campaign has:
- one clear business goal
- simple creator requirements
- verifiable steps
- believable payout logic
- a reason to repeat if successful

---

## 4. Operational Discipline Skill

### Objective
Run the platform with low ambiguity and low support chaos.

### Required Competence
- define milestone states clearly
- create standard response templates
- identify operational exceptions quickly
- maintain settlement trust
- resolve disputes without destabilizing both sides

### What Good Looks Like
Good ops feel:
- predictable
- fair
- visible
- low-drama
- rule-based

---

## 5. Data Interpretation Skill

### Objective
Use limited early data intelligently without overfitting or self-deception.

### Required Competence
- separate signal from noise
- compare creator cohorts fairly
- understand what “success” means in each vertical
- measure retention correctly
- identify whether repeat use is genuine or forced

### Key Metrics to Watch
- merchant repeat rate
- creator repeat completion rate
- campaign fill rate
- standby response rate
- campaign time-to-fill
- payout completion speed
- dispute rate
- creator progression rate
- on-platform repeat collaboration rate

---

## 6. Anti-Cliché Positioning Skill

### Objective
Tell the story in a way that signals originality and seriousness.

### Required Competence
- avoid lazy platform analogies
- frame the company around outcomes and infrastructure
- articulate why this is not just another marketplace
- explain the moat in operational and economic terms

→ See [Doc 01, Section 4] for the authoritative treatment of Push's non-cliché positioning, including the core framing and language to use in external communications.

---

## 7. Retention Architecture Skill

### Objective
Retain high-quality users by making platform participation economically compounding.

### Required Competence
- create meaningful progression
- ensure higher tiers unlock real value
- design creator incentives that extend beyond freebies
- make merchant repeat behavior easier on-platform than off-platform

### What Good Looks Like
Retention is strong when:
- creators see improved earnings over time
- merchants get faster and better campaign performance
- off-platform behavior feels less rational than on-platform behavior

---

## 8. Risk Management Skill

### Objective
Recognize danger early rather than responding after damage is visible.

### Required Competence
- identify fraud patterns, weak verticals, creator opportunism, merchant churn risk
- know when a campaign type is structurally bad
- distinguish operational problems from strategic ones

→ See [Doc 04] for the authoritative risk register, prioritization framework, and defense playbook that operationalizes these skills.

---

## 9. Founder Operating Skill

### Objective
Act as the system integrator across product, ops, and GTM.

### Required Competence
- talk to merchants directly
- understand creator psychology
- translate user pain into structured rules
- make hard prioritization calls
- resist ornamental feature temptation

### Founder Mistakes to Avoid
- building for aesthetics before economics
- expanding before density
- adding social features too early
- confusing signup growth with useful supply
- overvaluing brand language over execution reliability

---

## 10. Final Execution Standard

Push will only become a strong company if the team can do four things extremely well:

1. acquire the right merchants
2. recruit and shape the right creator supply
3. verify and route value better than ad hoc alternatives
4. convert repetition into data, trust, and lock-in

That is the real skill stack behind the business.
