THE CORE IDEA

Local creators as a customer acquisition channel — structured, verified, and repeatable.

The Problem

Merchants can't judge creator quality beyond follower count. Creators can't prove their real-world impact. Both sides operate on trust and hope, not data. Private deals mean no records, no accountability, no growth.

  • Creator quality is opaque
  • ROI cannot be measured
  • Campaigns cannot repeat reliably
  • Off-platform deals leave no data trail
The Solution

PUSH structures every collaboration: merchant goals → campaign design → creator matching → execution → verification → settlement → repeat. Every step creates data. Data creates credit. Credit creates compounding value.

  • Structured campaigns with clear goals
  • Creator credit built from real performance
  • Attribution: did the campaign actually work?
  • Repeat booking becomes data-driven

PUSH Master Strategy

Push is a local creator performance infrastructure layer that helps merchants acquire customers through measurable creator-driven demand.

Core Problem

Merchant Problem

Cannot judge creator quality, compare creators beyond followers, run structured campaigns, or avoid wasting money on low performers.

Creator Problem

Lack pricing power, verifiable performance records, upward mobility, and a stable path from free samples to paid work.

Market Problem

The creator economy has abundant supply, but it is not structured into a repeatable acquisition channel for local commerce.

5 Strategic Pillars

1. Offer OS: Convert merchant intent into structured campaigns (launch item, drive off-peak traffic, fill event attendance).

2. Creator Credit: Value creators by completion rate, reliability, merchant satisfaction, repeat booking, dispute history.

3. Attribution: Verify via unique codes, check-in proof, merchant confirmation, content verification, redemption evidence.

4. Demand Surface: Real-time demand interface showing where opportunities are, when they close, who qualifies.

5. Liquidity Layer: Standby mechanism for slot fill rate, creator engagement, urgency, merchant confidence.

Company-Building Thesis

The end state is a new market layer for local creator-driven demand: standardize local creator acquisition, rank by performance, route toward better outcomes, compound proprietary data, create lock-in through access, trust, and workflow.

Push IS vs Is NOT

IS: A transaction system · A trust system · A creator credit system · A local demand orchestration system

IS NOT: A generic influencer marketplace · A local discovery app · A content community or reward app · A lightweight loyalty system

Strategic Red Flags

The company becomes weak if it behaves like: a sample-giveaway app, creator coupon engine, posting board, local discovery map with weak economics, or content engagement platform with shallow verification.

Strategic Decision Test

Every major decision should answer at least one positively: Does this improve merchant ROI clarity? Does this improve creator earning power or quality sorting? Does this deepen platform defensibility? Does this reduce fraud, operational friction, or repeat campaign cost? Does this make future transactions more likely to stay on-platform? If the answer is no, the idea is likely noise.

20x Workflow Architecture

The workflow should feel like a controlled economic system, not a manual project-management pipeline.

Design Principles

Fast to understand, hard to misuse, easy to repeat, measurable at every stage, resilient against drop-off and fraud, produces better data over time.

Merchant Workflow (6 Stages)

1. Onboarding: Business info + operational intelligence (demand windows, AOV, high-margin products, capacity).

2. Goal Selection: Drive visits, increase weekday traffic, launch item, boost event attendance, fill underperforming slots.

3. Campaign Structuring: Offer, payout, slots, dates, creator requirements, verification, approval rules.

4. Candidate Routing: Ranked by fulfillment history, local fit, category match, reliability.

5. Execution: Clear deliverables, locked schedule, 7 milestone states.

6. Review & Repeat: Result summary, repeat recommendation, next campaign seed.

7 Milestone States

Accepted → Scheduled → Visited → Proof Submitted → Content Published → Verified → Settled

Creator Workflow (5 Stages)

Onboarding → Opportunity Discovery → Application/Standby → Fulfillment → Settlement & Progression

20x vs Basic Flow

Basic: Post → Apply → Accept → Content → Pay?

20x Push: Goal → Structure → Route → Verify → Settle → Repeat

Pain Points & Root Cause Map

The strongest businesses correctly identify which complaints are structural and build around resolving them.

Merchant Pain Points

A. Cannot Judge Creator Quality: Merchants evaluate by followers and aesthetics (weak signals). Root: no normalized credit layer.

B. Unclear ROI: Cannot answer "did this creator bring real visits?" Root: no structured attribution.

C. Operational Friction: No time for outreach, negotiation, tracking. Root: ad hoc relationship work.

D. Fear of Being Burned: No-shows, low quality, take-and-disappear. Root: no consequence-backed trust.

Creator Pain Points

A. No Pricing Power: Cannot prove worth. Root: no standardized credit system.

B. Income Instability: Random deal flow, low-quality offers. Root: no structured pipeline.

C. Exploitative Low-End: Trapped in "free sample" loops. Root: no distinction between onboarding and growth.

D. Trust Asymmetry: Don't know if merchants pay fairly. Root: weak merchant accountability.

Platform Pain Points

A. Cold Start (two-sided dependency) · B. Fraud (weak verification) · C. Disintermediation (introduction-only perception) · D. Commoditization (under-invest in credit/attribution)

5 Root Causes

1. Lack of Structure  ·  2. Lack of Verification  ·  3. Lack of Repeatability  ·  4. Lack of Durable Economic Incentives  ·  5. Lack of Compounding Platform Data

Risk Register & Defense Playbook

Push wins by making the platform more economically rational than off-platform behavior.

Strategic Risks (R1–R3)

R1 Derivative Positioning: Position as infrastructure, not marketplace.

R2 Weak Wedge: Start with one dense area, 1–2 strong verticals.

R3 Feature Drift: Only build features improving ROI, progression, defensibility, trust.

Marketplace Risks (R4–R6)

R4 Cold Start: Founder-led density creation. Manual zone selection, curated onboarding, concentrated volume.

R5 Low Merchant Retention: Better templates, reporting, creator sorting, fill speed.

R6 Low Creator Retention: Strong progression, limited freebie dependency, clear earnings ladder.

Behavioral Risks (R7–R9)

R7 Disintermediation: Superior on-platform value (credit accumulation, workflow automation, faster payouts, standby access).

R8 Opportunist Supply: Gate opportunities behind performance history.

R9 Merchant Abuse: Track merchant behavior, build accountability.

Fraud Risks (R10–R12)

Fake proof, fake redemption, engagement inflation. Defense: timestamped uploads, geo checks, merchant verification, pattern analysis. De-emphasize vanity metrics.

Operational Risks (R13–R15)

Approval bottlenecks (SLA nudges), settlement delays (explicit rules), support explosion (exception-based ops).

Competitive (R16)

Large platforms may copy surface features. Defense: focused vertical depth, better workflow, stronger creator credit layer, better merchant repeat mechanics.

Risk Priority Matrix

Execution Skills Playbook

Push will succeed only if the team can: acquire right merchants, recruit right creators, verify/route value, and convert repetition into data, trust, and lock-in.

9 Core Skills

1. Merchant Acquisition: Identify merchants with visible acquisition need. Best targets: cafes, dessert shops, beauty studios, boutique fitness, pop-ups.

2. Creator Quality Sourcing: Distinguish aesthetic quality from business reliability. Avoid freebie opportunists.

3. Campaign Design: One clear goal, simple requirements, verifiable steps, believable payout, reason to repeat.

4. Operational Discipline: Clear milestone states, standard templates, quick exception ID. Ops feel predictable and rule-based.

5. Data Interpretation: 9 key metrics: merchant repeat rate, creator repeat completion, fill rate, standby response, time-to-fill, payout speed, dispute rate, progression rate, on-platform repeat rate.

6. Anti-Cliché Positioning: Frame around outcomes and infrastructure, not lazy platform analogies.

7. Retention Architecture: Make participation economically compounding. Off-platform must feel irrational.

8. Risk Management: Recognize danger early. Identify fraud patterns, weak verticals, churn risk.

9. Founder Operating: System integrator. Resist ornamental features. Avoid: aesthetics before economics, expanding before density, social features too early, confusing signup growth with useful supply.

Cold Start Execution Plan

Step-by-step execution for the first 12 weeks, starting from zero merchants and zero creators.

Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1–4)

Week 1: Zone selection (ONE neighborhood, 30+ indie F&B shops), scout 30 merchants, research 50 creators.

Week 2: 15 in-person merchant conversations during slow periods. Target: 8 soft commits, 5–6 firm.

Week 3: DM 50 micro-creators (1K–15K followers). Build website MVP (Next.js + Vercel + Supabase).

Week 4: Set up 5 campaigns manually. Do 1–2 yourself as dry run to refine the flow.

Phase 2: First Campaigns (Week 5–8)

Week 5–6: 5 merchants × 2–3 creators = 10–15 slots. Daily ops. Pay within 24h via Venmo/Zelle.

Week 7–8: Second batch. 2+ repeat merchants from batch 1. Track repeat rate, completion, time-to-fill.

Phase 3: Validate Repeat (Week 9–12)

Week 9–10: Scale to 15 merchants. Introduce basic creator scoring and standby notifications.

Week 11–12: Transition to paid ($49/campaign or $99/month). Go/No-Go decision based on 7 key metrics.

Budget: $1,500–$2,200 total

V0.1 Pricing Model

Price for learning, not for revenue. Remove friction, prove willingness to pay.

Phase 1: Free Launch (Week 1–8)

$0 for merchants. Creator compensation: Standard $25–35, Premium $40–50, Story-only $15–20. Push pays from own pocket to build data.

Phase 2: Introductory Paid (Week 9–12)

Recommended: $99/month Basic (3 campaigns), $199/month Pro (8 campaigns). Creates habit and recurring revenue signal.

Payment Flow

Phase 1–2: Manual (Venmo/Zelle/Stripe invoice). Phase 3+: Automated via Stripe Connect with 15% take rate.

V0.1 Creator Scoring Model

Simple enough for spreadsheets, meaningful enough to predict outcomes, transparent enough for creators, resistant to gaming.

Composite Formula

Push Score = (Completion × 0.30) + (Reliability × 0.20) + (Quality × 0.25) + (Satisfaction × 0.15) + (Engagement × 0.10)

Score Dimensions

DimensionWeightWhat It Measures
Completion30%Campaigns completed / accepted. Highest weight — non-completion is worthless to merchants.
Quality25%Merchant content rating 1–5.
Reliability20%On-time submissions and no-show rate.
Satisfaction15%Merchant rebook score 1–5.
Engagement10%Post engagement rate (most gameable — lowest weight).

Tier System

TierPush ScoreCampaigns RequiredUnlocks
NewN/A0Basic campaigns, max 2 active
Rising50+3+All standard campaigns, standby eligible
Reliable70+8+Priority matching, premium campaigns, higher payout
Elite85+20+Top-tier campaigns, highest payouts, featured profile

Score Decay

Active (1+ campaign/30d): No decay · 30–60d inactive: −2 pts/month · 60–90d: −5 pts/month · 90d+: Frozen, marked inactive

Anti-Gaming Measures

Gaming AttemptDetectionPrevention
Only accepting easy campaignsTrack difficulty distributionRequire mix for tier promotion
Fake engagement (buying likes)Engagement spike analysisEngagement only 10% of score
Colluding with friendly merchantsRepeated pairing detectionFlag pairs with >3 consecutive
Rushing low-quality contentMerchant content ratingQuality is 25% of score

Cold Start Handling

First 3 campaigns are probationary. Provisional score starts at 60. Strong early performance triggers fast-track to Rising tier.
