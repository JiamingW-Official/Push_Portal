(02 · THE PROBLEM)

Marketing for local businesses is mostly guessing.

Push exists because three things broke at once, and nobody fixed the seam where they meet.

01

iOS 14 broke the link.

When Apple turned on App Tracking Transparency in 2021, pixel-based attribution lost roughly 40% of its accuracy overnight. For local merchants, whose conversion happens at a counter not a checkout page, the link was never clean to begin with. Today they buy Reach and Impressions and hope.

02

Last-touch attribution lies.

When a customer walks in saying "I saw you on Instagram," Instagram gets 100% of the credit. The food creator who actually started the journey three weeks earlier with a single reel gets nothing. There is no system that tells the truth about what brought a real person through a real door.

03

Creators get paid for the wrong thing.

A 50,000-follower influencer with a 2% conversion rate makes ten times more from brand deals than a 5,000-follower neighborhood foodie who actually drives walk-ins. The market pays for vanity, not value. The creators who actually drive customers slowly stop posting.

(03 · HOW IT WORKS)

One QR code closes the loop.

From a creator's post to a verified row in our database. Five steps. Thirty seconds. No app downloads, no behavioral tracking, no cookies.

  1. Step 01
    Mia posts. Push generates a unique QR per creator × merchant × campaign — no duplicates anywhere on the network. Mia drops it in a reel, story, TikTok, or in-person meetup.
  2. Step 02
    A fan scans. The QR opens a clean landing page: "Mia recommended this. Here's 15% off your first matcha." One tap, no account, no cookie. Consent + delete-link visible on the same page.
  3. Step 03
    They walk in. The geofence pings as the device crosses into the store. Attribution to Mia is now provisional — held server-side for 24 hours, no client-side tracking required.
  4. Step 04
    Checkout scan. The cashier scans the same QR at the register. ConversionOracle™ runs computer vision on the receipt, OCR on the order, geofence on the device. Three signals must agree. 3-of-3 counts; 2-of-3 goes to manual review.
  5. Step 05
    Truth is recorded. A 32-field row lands in push_transactions: who, what, when, where, how much, signal score. Mia gets paid that night. Sarah sees the ROI in the morning. Push trains the next version of Oracle.

ConversionOracle™ verification stack: 88% auto-pass, <20% manual review, <1.5% false positive — built across two years on a 37,000-transaction fraud dataset. Replicating it isn't a weekend project.

Two years ago Oracle's unit cost would have eaten the entire take rate. This year, verification costs 0.02% of the transaction. The thing that gates ConversionOracle is finally on our side.
